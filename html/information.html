Dr. Babasaheb Ambedkar

Dr. Bhimrao Ramji Ambedkar (1891–1956), also known as Babasaheb Ambedkar, was an Indian jurist, economist, social reformer, and politician who played a key role in drafting the Indian Constitution.

Early Life

Born on April 14, 1891, in Mhow (now in Madhya Pradesh), he faced caste-based discrimination from a young age but went on to become the first Dalit to earn a doctorate in economics abroad.

Contributions

  • Chief architect of the Indian Constitution
  • Fought for Dalit rights and social justice
  • Founded the Republican Party of India
  • Converted to Buddhism in 1956, inspiring millions to do the same

Legacy

Dr. Ambedkar is remembered as the Father of the Indian Constitution and a champion of human rights. His teachings continue to inspire people fighting against inequality.

“Cultivation of mind should be the ultimate aim of human existence.” – Dr. B. R. Ambedkar
